# Copy voice — Khadija's carousel tone

## The elevator pitch for her voice

Khadija is Head of Growth at Digigram. She writes like someone who has built the systems she's describing and broken them enough times to know which parts actually matter. The voice sits at the intersection of "operator who ships" and "teacher who distills." No hype, no jargon without a reason, no promises she can't deliver.

## Rules of thumb

- **Show the work, don't sell the outcome.** "Here's the exact stack" beats "unlock explosive growth."
- **Use real numbers, always.** `>20%`, `<5m`, `1h`, `95%`, `2.3%`. Rounded-off numbers feel honest. Over-precise numbers feel faked.
- **Name the tool.** If she built it in Apollo, say Apollo. If it's a Google Sheet, say so. Vagueness reads as bluffing.
- **Short sentences on slides, full thoughts in the takeaway box.** Headlines should fit in 2–3 lines at 72–78px. If you need more, it's a subhead, not a headline.
- **One accent word per headline.** Two max on the cover and close page.

## Hook headline patterns that have landed

**Contrast setup** — "Stop X. Start Y."
- "Stop sending more. Start filtering better."
- "Stop guessing ICP. Start watching signals."

**Category reframe** — take a known term and redefine it.
- "Turn anonymous traffic into real pipeline."
- "Cold outbound is a signal problem, not a volume problem."

**Cost of inaction** — call out what everyone else is still doing wrong.
- "Most teams still play the forms game."
- "You're leaking 95% of your real buying intent."

**Numbered promise** — be specific about the count.
- "The 5-signal stack that replaces cold outbound."
- "3 routing rules that doubled our meeting rate."

**Timeboxed authority** — show the investment is small.
- "1 hour of prospecting a week. Nothing more."
- "Under 5 minutes from signal to first touch."

## Subhead patterns

Subheads are one sentence, 15–20px Inter, muted color. They answer "what is this and why should I care" in plain language.

- "A signal-based inbound system. Built in Apollo."
- "The exact stack I use to close faster, with less noise."
- "If this helped, the next six episodes go deeper." *(only if she's actually publishing a series — remove otherwise)*

## Takeaway box copy

Every content page ends with a takeaway box. Format:

```
TAKEAWAY:   [1–2 sentences that distill the page into an operator-level insight]
```

Examples:

- "Scale isn't about collecting more forms. It's about seeing who's already watching."
- "The fastest inbound teams don't write better emails. They route faster."
- "Enrichment only pays off when it triggers a next action in the same minute."

Takeaways should feel like the line a reader would screenshot and quote back.

## CTA copy — close page

Only three actions, in this order:

1. **Like** — "Like if this was useful"
2. **Follow** — "Follow for more growth systems"
3. **Save** — "Save so you can come back to it"

Do not invent additional CTAs (no "DM me SIGNALS", no "comment below", no "subscribe to my newsletter") unless she explicitly asks for them.

## What to avoid

- Buzzwords without substance: "unlock", "supercharge", "game-changing", "revolutionary", "disruptive"
- Empty engagement bait: "which one resonates?" / "agree or disagree?"
- Fake scarcity: "only 5 spots left" / "limited time"
- LinkedIn-influencer broetry (single words on single lines for dramatic effect)
- Overpromising outcomes: "guaranteed to triple your pipeline"
- Fake credentials in the copy itself — her name and title are in the header, don't repeat them inside
